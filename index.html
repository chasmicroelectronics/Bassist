Chris Tiberius Heald - Bass Player & BV's

Summary

If you want a reliable, solid low-ender, look no further! Chris is a dedicated rock, pop and metal bass player with posh gear and genre-crossing talents.

Equipment - all the gear...

Chris plays a Wood n Tronic Aileron 6 string headless, Shuker Jazz 5 string fretless, a Warwick Thumb 6 string and a Peavey C5 passive (5 string) though a TC electronic Spectra comp, MXR DI+ into a Genz Benz STL9.2 and out of a pair of Vanderkley 1x12 cabs for a tectonic 1000W (but he can also play quietly). All of which fits in his own vehicle, that he can drive all by himself.

Repertoire - the greatest hits

Outside of various originals bands, Chris has performed in function bands covering a large number of rock, pop and club standards, etc. From classic rock such as Deep Purple, Whitesnake and Rainbow through to Muse and Audioslave on the modern side and the likes of Cher, Patti Smith. Tina Turner, Toto in the pop genre.

Background - an origin story

From humble beginnings as a classical guitarist, Chris got to university and realised electric guitarists were plentiful but bass players were nowhere to be found. Grabbing a terrible bass guitar from a second hand shop, he set about moving his classical guitar techniques to bass and co-founded a pop-punk sensation, legends in their own lunchbox, Disinformation, in Loughborough at the turn of the millennium. He soon grew out of the £40 knock-off Squire P-bass and entered the alluring world of GAS, building a stable of 4, 5 and ultimately 6 string basses.

Chris’ journey post-graduation took him up the M1 to the Steel City, Sheffield. Here he played in a couple of cover bands, an interesting if not especially long-lived Folk Metal outfit, and finally landed, in 2009, in local Prog-rockers Firegarden. That 6th string soon came in handy and he’s been holding down the low end ever since.

More recently Chris joined function band Patriots UK to bring rock and pop to the WMC’s of Yorkshire and the North-East, and with it more BV duties and a regular workout rigging the PA to his stable of talents.
